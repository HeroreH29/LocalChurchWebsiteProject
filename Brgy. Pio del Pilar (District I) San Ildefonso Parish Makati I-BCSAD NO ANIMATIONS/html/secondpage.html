History & Location
How we Started

In the year 1781, the Jesuits were the first to bring the images of San Ildefonso, San Ignatius de Loyola and San Pascual Baylon to Barrio de Culi-Culi. San Ildefonso was built in the year 1870 as a naval chapel and was built until it became an adobe land in Ayala y Cia. Archbishop Gabriel Reyes established it as a parish on 11 August 1951. The first to become a Parish Priest was Fr. Emilio Mercado who is a secular priest. The management was translated into the Salesians of Don Bosco on September 14, 1955 by Archbishop Rufino Santos and Fr, Aloysius Ferrari, then SDB-Provincial Superior. Si Fr. John Righetti, SDB and first Salesiano Primary School. Si Fr. John Ma. Rizatto, SDB and Parish School from 1957 to 1988, on August 15, 1964, the day of the Rapture of Mary and the 25th year priest of Fr. Rizatto. In his leadership he transformed Culi-Culi from the place of sin to the Christian Community. The name of the place was also changed to Pio del Pilar. Surrounding the Church are the Stained Glass depicting the Mystery of the Rosary, the Dream of St. John Bosco when he was Nine Years Old and the Gift of Saint Ildefonso from the Blessed Virgin Mary. The bell has four bells and the third has the engraved "LET US LOVE AND IMITATE THE MOST BLESSED VIRGIN MARY, MOTHER OF GOD AND OUR MOTHER"

Goals

1. At first it is important for us to bring young people across the country and the world together, to take part in youth activities that are linked with the style of working through the spirit of Don Bosco. We want to organize international initiatives that contribute to the full development of young people. It is necessary thereby to broaden the opportunities for young people who are, by reasons of various kinds, excluded from these kinds of activities.

2. Furthermore we strive for a profound exchange of information, ideas and experiences between the different partners. We want to facilitate contact, and deepen the understanding between the member organisations so more joint activities can take place.

3. And last, but not least, we want to represent the voice of the Don Bosco young people and defend their interests at an international level.
